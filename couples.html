Couples Therapy

Enter a safe space where your relationship deepens, communication flourishes, and mutual understanding grows. We blend empathy with evidence-based techniques to guide couples toward lasting connection and harmony.

Who Benefits

Couples experiencing one or more of the following:

  • Frequent misunderstandings or escalating conflicts
  • Emotional distance or fading intimacy
  • Difficulty navigating life transitions (marriage, parenthood, relocation)
  • Trust issues, jealousy, or recurring resentments
  • Desire to strengthen communication and rebuild partnership

How We Guide You

Our structured yet flexible approach ensures each couple feels heard, respected, and empowered to change:

  1. Comprehensive Assessment Each partner completes a relationship inventory and meets individually with Dr Lydia to clarify needs, goals, and strengths.
  2. Collaborative Goal-Setting Together, you define clear objectives—improved listening habits, renewed trust, or conflict-resolution skills.
  3. Tailored Intervention Plan Dr Lydia maps out targeted sessions, integrating proven models like Gottman Method exercises, Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) strategies, and narrative reshaping.
  4. Ongoing Feedback Loop At every milestone, you and Dr Lydia review progress, adjust tools, and celebrate breakthroughs, ensuring therapy stays aligned with your evolving relationship.

What to Expect in a Session

Each 60-minute session follows a predictable flow to maximize growth and maintain focus:

  1. Check-In (10 minutes): Share wins, concerns, or shifts since your last meeting.
  2. Core Exercise (30 minutes): Practice communication drills, role-reversals, or conflict-resolution techniques under guidance.
  3. Insight Discussion (15 minutes): Reflect on emotions, triggers, and patterns illuminated by the exercise.
  4. Homework Assignment (5 minutes): Set a small, actionable task—daily appreciation notes, active-listening practice, or a shared ritual.

Core Techniques

  • I-Statements & Reflective Listening: Cultivate empathy through non-judgmental dialogue.
  • Attachment Mapping: Identify and heal insecure bonds to foster safety.
  • Reframing Narratives: Transform “blame stories” into shared problem-solving perspectives.
  • Ritual Building: Establish consistent habits that nurture intimacy and fun.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many sessions are typical?

Most couples see meaningful change within 8–12 sessions, but plans remain flexible.

Can we invite family members?

Therapy focuses on your partnership; involving others is arranged only if it serves your goals.

Are virtual sessions available?

Yes—secure video conferencing allows you to work with Dr Lydia from anywhere.
