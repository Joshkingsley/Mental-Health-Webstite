Group Counselling with Dr Lydia N.

Enter a collaborative circle where individuals facing similar challenges come together to learn, heal, and grow. Dr Lydia N. blends systemic group dynamics with evidence-based methods to foster support, shared insight, and collective resilience.

Who Benefits

Participants who seek to:

  • Connect with peers experiencing similar life or relationship challenges
  • Gain new perspectives through shared stories and feedback
  • Build coping skills in a supportive community setting
  • Enhance communication, empathy, and social confidence
  • Receive structured guidance while benefiting from the group’s collective wisdom

How Dr Lydia Guides You

Dr Lydia’s group counselling model combines structure and flexibility to ensure every voice is valued:

  1. Group Intake & Assessment Each member completes a brief intake form and meets briefly one-on-one with Dr Lydia to outline personal goals and any confidentiality needs.
  2. Establishing Group Norms Together, you co-create guidelines—confidentiality, respectful listening, and turn-taking—to build a safe container.
  3. Thematic Module Delivery Sessions revolve around core themes (communication, boundaries, self-esteem, conflict resolution) using exercises drawn from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), psychodrama, and mindfulness.
  4. Peer Feedback & Reflection Dr Lydia facilitates structured feedback loops where members practice reflective listening and offer constructive support.
  5. Ongoing Monitoring & Adaptation At regular intervals, she reviews group progress, revises themes based on emerging needs, and celebrates collective milestones.

What to Expect in a Session

Each 90-minute group gathering follows a consistent flow:

  1. Welcome & Check-In (15 minutes): Each member shares a brief update or intention for the session.
  2. Focused Module (40 minutes): Interactive lesson—role-plays, guided imagery, or skill drills tied to the session’s theme.
  3. Group Dialogue (25 minutes): Members discuss insights, challenges, and personal applications of the exercise.
  4. Closing & Action Steps (10 minutes): Summarize key learnings and assign a small take-home practice.

Core Focus Areas

  • Building assertive communication and active listening skills
  • Developing emotional regulation and mindfulness practices
  • Establishing healthy boundaries in relationships
  • Enhancing self-esteem and social confidence
  • Conflict resolution through collaborative problem-solving
  • Coping strategies for anxiety, stress, and life transitions

Frequently Asked Questions

How many participants are in each group?

Groups are limited to 8–12 members to maintain safety and engagement.

What’s the recommended duration of attendance?

We suggest committing to 8–12 weekly sessions for meaningful progress.

Can I join mid-cycle?

Yes—as long as you complete an intake and orientation with Dr Lydia.

Are virtual group sessions available?

Secure video conferencing options are offered for remote participation.
